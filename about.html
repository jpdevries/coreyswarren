By nurture or by nature, design is woven into the daily tapestry of my life. When I wake up in the morning, I’m looking though the design goggles. Graphics, architecture, photography, information systems, civil infrastructure, clothing and products all pique my interest. Impulsively and scrupulously, I judge these creations for their value, aesthetic appeal, and functionality. I greatly admire creations such as the Apple computer, alpine climbing gear, and the bicycle. Humans like Herb Lubalin and Milton Glaser inspire my process and form. Kolomon Moser and Josef Hoffmann showed me totality in design. Mark Zuckerberg and Steve Jobs set the precedent in product and marketing innovation. I am an observer and a prism; a sum total of every one of my experiences.

I am grateful for the education I received as an adoledcent at the Community School in Sun Valley, Idaho. The outdoor classroom, language and writing skills, and strong student-teacher relationships laid the groundwork for my current personal and professional identity. Four colleges in three states provided me a variety of educational experiences before I set out to write the most significant chapter of my odyssey to date.

Following my graduation in 2008, I embarked on an adventure in business with my good friend, and over the next five years developed our idea into a cultural staple in our town of Bellingham, WA. With all odds against us, we persisted to create INNATE; a hub for retail, art, music and fashion, based around the idea of discovering and celebrating one’s “innate” abilities and capacity for creative expression. We monetized our art and craft through commercial screen printing, graphic design, and marketing our t-shirt brand, while simultaneously creating community through meme. Building and steering our own ship, we established an identity and gainful employment, and learned invaluable lessons of risk, reward, and relationships.

Now at twenty seven years old, I am hungry for the opportunity to contribute my learning to a successful creative team working on high energy, meaningful work. I am a creative problem solver, and I offer your firm a highly capable, compassionate team player with the humbleness to learn and the tenacity to grow.

— Corey Warren
